FOR JOB SEEKERS

Maryland is the national epicenter of cybersecurity activities, but the industry is facing a critical shortage of workers. State-of-the-art job training in cybersecurity and information assurance is available to eligible job seekers to increase the pipeline of qualified workers in this high-demand field. The Cybersecurity Consortium has dedicated staff to work with job seekers to enter training and connect to employment opportunities.



Eligibility Requirements

You must be 18 or older, seeking full-time employment within one year of certification, and be one of the following:

  • Unemployed - unemployed or laid off, and seeking skills to obtain employment in the industry.

  • Underemployed- currently employed part-time or in a low wage job, OR

  • Incumbent Worker -currently employed in the IT field, and seeking to upgrade skills.



Veterans will get priority of service and will need to show documentation



To succeed in training, program participants should have one or more of the following:

  • Education - A or higher degree in Computer Science or other computer-based concentration.

  • Certification - Graduation from a certificate program in IT or computer science.

  • Education/Experience - BS/BA degree in any field plus 2 years of direct IT work experience.

  • Experience -3 years IT experience, and some computer-based college courses.



Veterans & Wounded Warriors No educational and/or work experience requirement. Client must still meet the community college training partner's educational level requirements for taking credit courses, to be eligible for the Pathways to Cybersecurity Careers Consortium Program.

Before applying for training in the Pathways to Cybersecurity Careers program, individuals should consider potential employment barriers to successful completion of the program and securing employment in cybersecurity.



Steps to Enter Cybersecurity Program

  1. Contact your local One-Stop Career Center.

  2. Attend One-Stop Orientation.

  3. Cyber Career Advisor will contact you to set up a time to meet for eligibility evaluation and to bring in documentation.

  4. Meet with Cyber Career Advisor to finalize Cybersecurity paperwork to enter program.
